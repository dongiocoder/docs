# Quickstart

This guide walks you through setting up your first context hierarchy in XOOS.

## Prerequisites

- Access to a XOOS company account
- At least one support channel to model (chat, email, or voice)

## Step 1: Create a Workstream

Workstreams represent your support channels at the highest level.

1. Navigate to **Context Engineering** > **Workstreams**
2. Click **Create Workstream**
3. Fill in the details:
   - **Name**: A descriptive name (e.g., "Customer Support Chat")
   - **Type**: Inbound, Outbound, or Background
   - **Channel**: The communication channel (chat, email, voice, etc.)
   - **Platform**: The CX platform you use (Zendesk, Intercom, etc.)
4. Click **Create**

## Step 2: Add Contact Drivers

Contact drivers represent the common reasons customers reach out.

1. Click on your workstream to view its details
2. Navigate to the **Contact Drivers** tab
3. Click **Add Contact Driver**
4. Provide:
   - **Name**: The contact reason (e.g., "Order Issues")
   - **Description**: A detailed explanation of when this applies
   - **Integrations**: Any systems involved (optional)
5. Click **Save**

## Step 3: Define Sub-Scenarios

Sub-scenarios are specific situations within each contact driver.

1. Click on a contact driver to view its details
2. Navigate to the **Sub-Scenarios** tab
3. Click **Add Sub-Scenario**
4. Enter:
   - **Name**: The specific scenario (e.g., "Cancel Order")
   - **Description**: When and how this scenario applies
5. Click **Save**

## Step 4: Create Step Guides

Step guides provide procedural instructions for handling each scenario.

1. Navigate to **Knowledge** > **Step Guides**
2. Click **Create Step Guide**
3. Add:
   - **Title**: Clear, action-oriented title
   - **Description**: Summary of what this guide covers
   - **Content**: Step-by-step instructions using the editor
4. Click **Save**

## Step 5: Link Everything Together

Connect your step guides to sub-scenarios:

1. Open a sub-scenario
2. Click **Link Step Guide**
3. Select the appropriate guide
4. The AI agent will now use this guide when handling this scenario
