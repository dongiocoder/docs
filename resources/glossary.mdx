# Glossary

Key terms and definitions used in XOOS Context Engineering.

## A

### AHT (Average Handle Time)
The average time taken to resolve a customer contact, including talk time, hold time, and after-contact work.

### AI Agent
An automated system powered by artificial intelligence that can handle customer inquiries using the context you've configured in XOOS.

## C

### Channel
The communication medium for customer interactions: chat, email, voice, messaging, social, etc.

### Contact Driver
A category representing a primary reason customers contact support. Sits between workstreams and sub-scenarios in the hierarchy.

### Context Engineering
The practice of organizing support knowledge into a structured hierarchy that AI agents can understand and use effectively.

### Context Hierarchy
The three-level structure of Workstream → Contact Driver → Sub-Scenario used to organize support knowledge.

### CSAT (Customer Satisfaction)
A metric measuring customer satisfaction, typically collected via post-interaction surveys.

### Current Version
The active, editable version of a step guide. Historical versions are frozen.

## F

### FCR (First Contact Resolution)
The percentage of customer inquiries resolved on the first interaction without requiring follow-up.

## H

### Historical Version
A frozen, read-only snapshot of a previous step guide version.

## I

### Inbound
Customer-initiated contacts where the support team responds to incoming requests.

### Integration
A connection to an external system (CRM, order management, etc.) used in handling customer inquiries.

## K

### Knowledge Base
The collection of reference documents, policies, and informational content in XOOS.

### Knowledge Document
A reference document in the knowledge base (support article, SOP, policy, or template).

## M

### Metrics
Performance measurements tracked at workstream, contact driver, and sub-scenario levels (volume, AHT, FCR, CSAT, etc.).

## O

### Outbound
Company-initiated contacts where the support team proactively reaches out to customers.

## P

### Platform
A customer experience (CX) software system like Zendesk, Intercom, or Gladly.

### Policy
A type of knowledge document containing official company guidelines and rules.

## Q

### QA Score (Quality Assurance Score)
A metric based on internal evaluation of agent performance against quality criteria.

## S

### SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)
A type of knowledge document containing internal procedures for specific operations.

### Step Guide
A procedural document with step-by-step instructions for handling specific scenarios. Supports versioning.

### Sub-Scenario
The most specific level of the context hierarchy, representing exact situations within a contact driver.

### Support Article
A type of knowledge document containing customer-facing help content.

## T

### Tags
Keywords applied to knowledge documents for organization and searchability.

### Template
A type of knowledge document containing pre-written content for common situations.

## V

### Version
A snapshot of a step guide's content at a point in time. Versions are numbered sequentially (v1, v2, v3).

### Version Comment
A required explanation of what changed when creating a new step guide version.

### Visibility
A document setting controlling whether content is internal-only or can be shared with customers.

### Volume
The number of contacts handled, typically measured monthly.

## W

### Workstream
The top-level organizational unit in Context Engineering, representing a distinct support channel or function.
